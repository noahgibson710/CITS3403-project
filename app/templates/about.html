About

Our calculator provides precise estimates of your daily macronutrient requirements (protein, carbohydrates, and fats) to help you reach your fitness goals. Whether you're trying to lose weight, build muscle, or maintain a healthy lifestyle, understanding your macros is key.

How It Works

By entering your age, weight, height, activity level, and fitness goals, our calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation to determine your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) — the number of calories your body burns at rest.

Mifflin-St Jeor Equation:
For men: BMR = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) − 5 × age + 5
For women: BMR = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) − 5 × age − 161

This BMR is then multiplied by your activity level to estimate your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) — the total calories you burn per day.

Activity Multipliers:
Sedentary (little or no exercise): BMR × 1.2
Lightly active (1–3 days/week): BMR × 1.375
Moderately active (3–5 days/week): BMR × 1.55
Very active (6–7 days/week): BMR × 1.725
Extra active (physical job + exercise): BMR × 1.9

Based on your goal (e.g. fat loss, muscle gain, or maintenance), the TDEE is adjusted to create a calorie deficit, surplus, or maintenance level.
